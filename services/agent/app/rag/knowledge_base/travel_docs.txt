Cabin baggage for domestic flights in India is usually limited to 7 kg with dimensions not exceeding 55x35x25 cm.

Passengers should arrive at least 2 hours before domestic flight departure and 3 hours before international flights.

Valid government photo ID such as Aadhaar card, passport, voter ID, or driving license is mandatory for domestic air travel in India.

Web check-in is generally available 48 hours before departure and closes 2 hours before the flight.

Refund policies depend on fare type. Refundable tickets allow cancellation with minimal charges while non-refundable tickets may only refund taxes.

Boarding gates close 25 minutes before domestic flight departure. Passengers must be at the gate before this time.

Check-in baggage allowance for domestic flights is typically 15 kg for economy class and 25 kg for business class.

Infants under 2 years can travel on a parent's lap with a discounted fare and are allowed one piece of cabin baggage up to 7 kg.

Airlines like IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet, Vistara, and AirAsia India operate domestic routes across India.

Indian Railways operates one of the largest rail networks in the world with over 7000 stations across the country.

IRCTC is the official website and app for booking Indian Railways tickets online. Registration requires a valid email and mobile number.

Tatkal ticket booking opens at 10:00 AM for AC classes and 11:00 AM for non-AC classes, one day before the journey date.

Indian Railways offers multiple classes: 1AC (First AC), 2AC (Second AC), 3AC (Third AC), Sleeper (SL), Chair Car (CC), and General (GN).

Waitlisted railway tickets get confirmed based on cancellations. RAC (Reservation Against Cancellation) tickets allow boarding with shared berth.

PNR status can be checked on the IRCTC website, app, or by sending an SMS with the 10-digit PNR number to 139.

Railway ticket cancellation charges vary by class and time before departure. Cancelling a confirmed ticket more than 48 hours before gets a higher refund.

Senior citizens aged 60 and above are eligible for concession on Indian Railways tickets. The discount varies by class and gender.

Popular train routes in India include Delhi-Mumbai (Rajdhani Express, 16 hours), Chennai-Bangalore (Shatabdi Express, 5 hours), and Kolkata-Delhi (Duronto Express, 17 hours).

Platform tickets cost Rs 10 to Rs 50 depending on the station and are required for non-passengers entering the platform area.

Passengers can carry up to 40 kg of luggage in first class, 35 kg in second class, and 15 kg in sleeper class on Indian Railways.

State transport buses (KSRTC, APSRTC, TSRTC, MSRTC) operate across various Indian states with online booking available on their websites.

Private bus operators like RedBus, AbhiBus, and MakeMyTrip aggregate bus tickets from operators like VRL Travels, SRS Travels, and Orange Travels.

Bus types in India include ordinary, express, deluxe, semi-sleeper, sleeper, AC sleeper, Volvo AC, and multi-axle luxury coaches.

Bus luggage allowance is typically 15 kg per passenger. Excess luggage may be charged extra.

Overnight sleeper buses are popular for routes of 6 to 12 hours and offer berths similar to train sleeper class.

Bus ticket cancellation policies vary by operator. Most allow free cancellation up to 24 hours before departure with charges increasing closer to travel time.

Popular bus routes include Bangalore-Chennai (6 hours), Hyderabad-Bangalore (10 hours), Mumbai-Goa (10 hours), and Delhi-Jaipur (5 hours).

E-tickets for buses can be shown on mobile phones. No printout is required for most private operators.

Travel insurance is recommended for all modes of transport. Many booking platforms offer optional travel insurance at checkout.

During peak seasons like Diwali, Christmas, and summer holidays, booking tickets 30 to 60 days in advance is recommended for all transport modes.

Passengers with disabilities are entitled to special assistance and priority boarding on flights and reserved seats on trains.

Vande Bharat Express (Train 18) is India's fastest semi-high-speed train, operating on multiple routes with maximum speeds of 160 kmph. It features chair car and executive chair car classes with onboard catering.

The Rajdhani Express connects major cities to Delhi with fully air-conditioned coaches. It offers 1AC, 2AC, and 3AC classes with complimentary meals included in the fare.

For domestic flights in India, liquids in carry-on bags must be in containers of 100ml or less, placed in a single transparent resealable plastic bag of 1 litre capacity.

IndiGo is India's largest airline by market share, operating over 300 aircraft on domestic and international routes. It offers 6E Flex, 6E Prime, and basic economy fares.

Air India, now owned by Tata Group, operates both domestic and international flights. It offers economy, premium economy, and business class on select routes.

SpiceJet offers affordable fares on domestic routes with SpiceMax (extra legroom) and regular economy options. Web check-in is free on SpiceJet's website.

Passengers can book train tickets through IRCTC up to 120 days in advance. For high-demand routes, early booking is essential to secure confirmed berths.

The Indian Railways e-catering service allows passengers to order food from restaurants along the route. Orders can be placed on the IRCTC e-catering website or by calling 1323.

Ladies quota and lower berth quota are available on Indian Railways for women and senior citizens respectively. These can be selected during booking on IRCTC.

Ola and Uber operate in major Indian cities for airport transfers. Pre-paid taxi counters are available at most airports for fixed-rate rides to the city.

Major Indian airports like Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Bangalore (BLR), Hyderabad (HYD), and Chennai (MAA) have metro or rail connectivity to the city center.

For international travel from India, a valid passport with at least 6 months validity from the date of travel is required along with the destination country's visa.

Indian tourists can get visa-on-arrival or e-visa for many countries including Thailand, Sri Lanka, Maldives, Indonesia, Malaysia, and several others.

Food on Indian trains ranges from pantry car meals (Rs 50-200) to e-catering restaurant orders (Rs 100-500). Popular options include thali meals, biryani, and regional specialties.

Metro systems operate in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, Kochi, Jaipur, Lucknow, and Nagpur, providing affordable local transport with fares starting from Rs 10.

Prepaid SIM cards are available at Indian airports from Jio, Airtel, and Vi. A passport copy and passport-size photo are required. Most plans include data, calls, and SMS.

WiFi is available at most major Indian airports and some railway stations. Google Station provides free WiFi at 400+ railway stations across India.

The Konkan Railway route along India's western coast from Mumbai to Mangalore is considered one of the most scenic train journeys, passing through 2000+ bridges and 91 tunnels.

Budget accommodation in India ranges from Rs 500-1500 for hostels and budget hotels, Rs 1500-4000 for mid-range hotels, and Rs 4000+ for premium hotels. Booking platforms include OYO, MakeMyTrip, Goibibo, and Booking.com.

Travel medical insurance covering Rs 5-10 lakhs is recommended for domestic travel and Rs 25-50 lakhs for international travel from India.

EMI options are available on most travel booking platforms for flights and hotels. No-cost EMI is offered through credit cards on select booking amounts.

The Indian government's DigiYatra initiative allows facial recognition-based entry at select airports, reducing check-in and boarding time.

Uber and Ola intercity services operate between nearby cities. Prices are competitive with bus fares for groups of 2-3 people on routes under 300 km.

For overnight train journeys, upper berths (UB) are cheapest, lower berths (LB) most accessible, and middle berths (MB) in between. Side upper and side lower berths are shorter in length.

Group booking on Indian Railways allows booking up to 6 passengers in a single transaction. For groups larger than 6, a group booking request can be submitted to the railway division.

Volvo AC buses are the most popular premium bus type in India, featuring 2+2 seating, AC, charging points, and reclining seats. Prices are typically 30-50% more than ordinary buses.

The IRCTC tourist train packages include the Maharajas Express (luxury), Golden Chariot (South India), and Buddhist Circuit Train. These all-inclusive packages cover accommodation, meals, and sightseeing.
