Section 01

Executive Summary & Product Vision

YatraAI is a conversational AI travel booking platform that enables anyone — regardless of language, literacy, or technical ability — to book bus, train, and flight tickets through natural text or voice conversations. Built on Next.js with a voice-first, multilingual approach, it also proactively suggests affordable accommodation (hotels, dharamshalas, homestays) near the destination.

Vision Statement

"A new way. A better way. That becomes their way." — YatraAI exists to make travel booking as natural as talking to a friend. No forms. No dropdowns. No confusion. Just a conversation.

Core Differentiators

🗣️ Voice Call Booking

User calls a number → AI agent speaks in their language → books the ticket via conversation. No app or internet needed for initiation.

🌐 10+ Indian Languages

Hindi, Telugu, Tamil, Marathi, Bengali, Kannada, Gujarati, Malayalam, Punjabi, Odia + English + Hinglish/code-mixing support.

🏠 Dharamshala Layer

Budget stays near pilgrimage, medical, and government destinations — a uniquely Indian need no competitor addresses.

Section 02

Problem Statement & User Personas

Problem Statement

Booking a bus, train, or flight ticket in India requires navigating complex, English-heavy, form-based interfaces (IRCTC, MakeMyTrip, redBus). For elderly users, rural populations, non-English speakers, and the digitally underserved, this process is exclusionary. They depend on relatives, local agents, or physically visiting counters — losing time, money, and access.

User Personas

Persona 1: Lakshmi Devi (Age 65, Warangal, Telangana)

Language: Telugu only  |  Tech: Basic Android, uses WhatsApp for video calls  |  Travel: Visits Tirupati twice a year, grandchildren in Hyderabad

Pain: Cannot book IRCTC tickets herself. Waits for son to visit and book. Has missed medical appointments due to ticket unavailability. Does not understand English interface.

Need: Speak to someone in Telugu → get a train ticket booked → receive confirmation on WhatsApp → find a dharamshala near the temple.

Persona 2: Ramesh Kumar (Age 28, Patna, Bihar)

Language: Hindi/Bhojpuri  |  Tech: Smartphone, uses Jio internet  |  Travel: Migrant worker, travels to Delhi/Mumbai for construction work

Pain: Booking agents charge ₹100-200 extra per ticket. IRCTC confuses him with OTP/Aadhaar steps. Gets waitlisted and doesn't know how to check PNR status.

Need: Type in Hindi on WhatsApp "Delhi ja raha hu 15 tarikh ko" → get options → pay via UPI → ticket done.

Persona 3: Priya Menon (Age 34, Kochi, Kerala)

Language: Malayalam/English  |  Tech: Tech-savvy, iPhone user  |  Travel: Plans family trips, coordinates between 4 family members

Pain: Juggles between 3-4 apps to book flight + train + hotel. Comparison shopping is exhausting. No unified trip view.

Need: Single conversation to plan family trip → get all bookings (flight to Jaipur, hotel, local transport) in one thread.
